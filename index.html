What if every time you ignored the alarm, it cost you shame or money? Would you continue to sleep in? The idea behind snoozePenalty™ is that when you snooze, it costs you more than the time you obviously wanted in the morning.

To start using snoozePenalty™, you setup your account and link it to either your Facebook or Twitter accounts. The first time you snooze an alarm, a message will be posted telling everyone that you ignored your alarm. The penalties escalate from there.

For those with severe snoozing problems, things get interesting when you choose to deposit into your penalty fund. Once linked to a form of payment, snoozePenalty™ can be configured to deduct from your balance a penalty that you specify.

Simply want to get better at waking up on tiem and need that little extra motivation? Set the penalty lower. Its all in your hands

Have an important meeting in the morning that you cannot be late for? How about that final exam which determines whether or not you can gratuate? Up the ante, and set the penalty higher.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where does the money go when I set a penalty?
When you configure a penalty amount, every time you snooze, we will deduct the balance which goes toward paying for hosting, development, and other business expenses.
Can I stop the social alerts or get my money back?
Any time! If you no longer want to use snoozePenalty™, simply use the 'Stop Shaming' and/or 'Refund' options from your account settings. All social alerts will cease, and if you choose to get a refund, any remaining balance will be returned to you.
When will snoozePenalty™ be ready?
Currently, the developers for snoozePenalty™ all have regular day jobs as well as other responsibilities, so development is done piece by piece. As soon as our funding goal is met, however more time can be dedicated to launching snoozePenalty™.
Will snoozePenalty™ work on Android? iOS? Windows Mobile?
The initial release of snoozePenalty™ is intended for the Android operating system. Based on demand, we intend to be able to offer an iOS verion of the application as well. Windows Mobile is currently not on our roadmap.
